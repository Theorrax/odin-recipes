Ossobuco

Description

Ossobuco is a specialty of cross-cut veal shakes braised with vegetable, white wine, and broth. It is either served with Gremolata, Risotto alla Milanese or polenta depending on the regional variance. The marrow the hole in the bone, a delicacy, is the main feature of the dish.

The two types of ossobuco are a modern version that features the use of tomatoes and the orginial version which does not. The older version, called ossobuco in bianco is flavored with cinnamon, bay leaf and Gremolata. The recipe featured today will be a modern version which features tomatoes, carrots, celery, and onion: gremolata is optional. While veal is the traditional meat used for ossobuco, dishes with other meats such as pork have also been called ossobuco.

Ingredients

  • 2lbs veal shank, cut into short length
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 large carrot, chopped
  • 2/3 cup dry white wine
  • 2/3 cup beef stock
  • 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • salt and pepper

Germolata

  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh flat leaf parsley
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 2 teaspoons grated lemon zest

Directions

  1. Dust the veal shank lightly with salt, pepper, and flour
  2. Melt butter over medium-high heat, add the veal, and cook until browned on the outside. Move to a plate.
  3. Add two cloves of crushed garlic and onion to the skillet; cook and stir until onion is tender.
  4. Return the veal to the pan and mix in the carrot and wine. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Pour in tomatoes and beef stock, and season with salt and pepper.
  6. Cover, and simmer over low heat for 1 1/2 hours, basting the veal every 15 minutes or so.
  7. In a small bowl, mix together parsley, 1 garlic clove, and lemon zest. Sprinkle the fresh Gremolata over the Ossobuco before serving.
