How to Make Korean BBQ

Korean BBQ comes in many diffrent styles. I've always been a fan of pork belly (I think it has the best flavor). However, you can also get beef short ribs, sirloin, or ribeye. But i've also seen chicken, namely thighs at korean bbq shops before. It hit or miss depending on the shop you choose to go, leaving your choices up to luck primarily. The beauity of Korean BBQ isn't the meat though it is tasty. The real beauity is in the Banchan, the number of sides seems limitless. My personal favorites are cucumber salad and kimchi. But this page is going to focus specifically on the meat and marinades. So without futher ado, here is the recipe for Korean BBQ.

Ingredients

Meats
  • 1 lb pork belly thinly sliced
  • 1 lb brisket thinly sliced sirloin or ribeye ok as well
  • 2 lb bone-in-short ribs Korean cut (thinly sliced) use 1/2 the amount if boneless
Marinade
  • 1 medium onion chopped, roughly 2 cups
  • 8 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger crushed
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce low sodium preferred
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 2 tbps rice vinegar
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp gochujang Korean hot pepper paste

Steps

  1. Well before you want to eat, make the marinade: In a blender, blend the onion, garlic, and ginger with 1/4 cup water, then combine the mix with the soy sauce, brown sugar, toasted sesame oil, gochujang, and black pepper. Divide your marinade in thirds marinating your meats for a minimum of 2 hours or overnight.
  2. Cook the meat on the hot plate/grill pan, flipping as needed. Enjoy!
